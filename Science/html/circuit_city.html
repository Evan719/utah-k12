Basic Circuits

Materials:



  • Emergency Flashlight Kit:
    • 3 Hershey Kisses
    • 1 pencil
    • 1 piece of woolen cloth
    • 1 D battery
    • 1 small bulb
    • 1 toothpick
  • Foam board or cardboard
  • Insulated Wire
  • Wire cutters/strippers
  • Electrical tape
  • Small bulbs (Christmas lights cut up)
  • D-cell batteries
  • Milk cartons
  • Electrical switches (two brads, paper clip on a small square of oak tag
  • Art supplies

For the Teacher:

Give the students an Emergency Flashlight Kit: a brown bag with the following items: three Hershey Kisses™ (or a candy bar wrapped in foil…beware, some are now wrapped in paper treated with silver, but are not foil), a pencil, a piece of woolen cloth, a D-cell battery, a toothpick, and a small bulb. Tell the students they must make an emergency flashlight using only the items in the bag.




STEP 1

Instructions



Have the students meet together to decide on the placement of their properties and what commercial building they are going to make. Then have each student draw up the circuit diagram for their individual property.



STEP 2

Instructions




Give each student a piece of foam board or cardboard to use as his or her property. This works best if each of the individual pieces has been cut from a larger piece so that they fit back together nicely. Make sure that one extra piece is reserved for the commercial building.





STEP 3

Instructions



Have the students each save two milk cartons from the cafeteria. One will be used for the house and the other as a cover for the power source. The group will also need a small cardboard box for their commercial building.





STEP 4

Instructions



Give each student two bulbs and two D-size batteries for their power source.


STEP 5

Instructions



Have lots of insulated wire, wire cutter/strippers, and electrical tape available for making circuits. They will also need to make a switch using two brads and a paper clip attached to a small piece of oak tag or cardboard.


STEP 6

Instructions



Have art supplies available for making their properties attractive (construction paper to cover the milk cartons, craft sticks for fences, Spanish moss for bushes, etc.).


STEP 7

Instructions



The students will then proceed to put a light through the bottom of their milk carton house, place the house on the board and run the wires underneath the board to complete the simple circuit. The power source should be placed near the switch and then covered with the other milk carton (cut the top part off and turn it over). The outside light source can be a lamppost (flexible drinking straws work well for this) or any other creative use of outside lighting (a porch light, light inside a hot tub, on a sport court, etc.).


STEP 8

Instructions



Once the students have completed their own properties, they make the commercial property and run the circuitry for two light sources.


STEP 9

Instructions



Once everyone has their subdivisions complete and put together, turn off the lights in your classroom and have the official “lighting” ceremony.


Discuss the results.

Questions



Why does current stop when the switch is open?

How could you make the light brighter?

How do circuit breakers/fuses work?
